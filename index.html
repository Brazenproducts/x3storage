Overview

Can-Am Maverick X3 owners face a persistent challenge: the machine is built for performance, not cargo. The rear cargo area is minimal compared to utility-focused UTVs. Serious trail riders and overlanders who take an X3 need smart storage solutions that add meaningful capacity without compromising the X3's balance, cage clearance, or safety systems. The best X3 storage solutions are engineered for the X3's specific cage geometry, rear rack dimensions, and weight distribution considerations. Generic UTV storage that works adequately on a Polaris General or Yamaha Wolverine won't fit the X3's rear cage architecture without significant modification — or it will, but will interfere with the X3's rear passenger space or cage systems.

Buying Guide — What to Know Before You Buy

X3 Weight Distribution and Storage

The X3's performance is sensitive to weight distribution. Heavy storage mounted high (on cage or roof) raises center of gravity — perceptible at X3 speeds. Keep heavy items low (under-seat, rear rack close to frame) and distribute weight symmetrically.

Weatherproofing Requirements for X3

The X3 regularly encounters water crossings where storage must survive submersion. Minimum weatherproofing standard: YKK-style weatherproof zippers and sealed seams. Genuine waterproofing (IPX6+ rated enclosures) is needed for electronics.

Access Speed in Trail Conditions

The best storage is the storage you can access quickly without stopping. Cage bags should have pull tabs accessible from the driver seat. Under-seat trays should slide out without tools. Evaluate access speed alongside capacity when choosing.

X3 Cage Geometry Specifics

X3 rear cage tube spacing is narrower than most other UTVs. Bags designed for 'most UTVs' often sag or require bungee supplementation on the X3. X3-specific bags from brands that publish tube spacing dimensions deliver consistently better fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much storage does an X3 have from the factory?

Very little — the X3 is performance-focused, not cargo-focused. The rear cargo area is approximately 8 liters without accessories. With cage bags and a rear cargo box, total accessible storage can reach 25–35 liters.

Can I run a roof rack on an X3?

Yes — X3 roof racks exist that mount to the factory cage mounting points. Capacity is typically 50 lbs. Weight on the roof affects X3 handling at high speeds — keep roof loads to lightweight items (tents, sleeping pads) and stay below the rated capacity.

Do X3 cage bags interfere with rear passengers?

X3-specific bags are cut to avoid rear passenger space. Generic UTV bags may extend into the rear passenger area on the X3's narrower rear cage. Verify dimensions before ordering for a 4-seat X3 Max configuration.

What's the best way to organize X3 trail essentials?

Tiered access: daily essentials (snacks, water, phone) in cage bags. Tools and recovery accessories in rear cargo box. First aid and emergency items in under-seat tray — always accessible, never buried under other gear.

Are X3 storage bags waterproof?

Quality X3 bags from Kemimoto and DragonFire use weatherproof (water resistant) zippers and seam sealing — adequate for rain and light water crossings. True waterproof submersion protection requires hard-sided cases with O-ring seals for valuable items like cameras and electronics.

Can I add a spare tire carrier to the X3 rear?

Yes — X3-specific rear tire carriers that mount to the factory cage points exist from brands like Tusk and SuperATV. They eliminate rear cargo space but allow carrying a matching spare tire for remote rides where a flat could end the trip.

How do I secure cargo in the X3 rear cargo area?

The factory rear rack has tie-down loops rated for cargo straps. Use ratchet cam straps rather than bungee cords for anything heavy. Bungee cords allow too much movement at X3 speeds. Secure loads symmetrically to maintain weight balance.

What's the weight limit for X3 cage bags?

X3 cage bags are limited by the cage mounting point strength and strap tension. Typical safe limits are 15–20 lbs per bag. Exceeding this with heavy gear can cause the bag to shift or pull mounting hardware loose over rough terrain.
